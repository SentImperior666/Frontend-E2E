# Journal entry — Stitch prompt template

## Layout

A reading-first layout — body text takes priority; chrome is minimal.

**Header** — entry title (display face, large), subtitle / category line, in-world date + real-world updated-at, author chip, tag row.

**Body** — single column, comfortable measure (~70ch). Drop-cap on the leading paragraph. Inline cross-reference links visually distinct (e.g., a different underline weight + a small marginal symbol). Footnotes break out to the right margin on wide viewports, fall inline with collapsible toggles on narrow viewports.

**Right margin (wide only)** — a "Related entries" rail showing 3–5 cross-referenced entries with thumbnails. Below: a "Last edited by" + edit history affordance.

**Footer** — comments / annotations area with a subtle separator. Each annotation: avatar, name, date, body.

Tags: dwarves, Order of Moradin, Ashen Peaks, frontier

Body:

Forgewatch was raised in the late summer of 982, carved into the southern flank of the Ashen Peaks as a forward keep for the Order of Moradin during the Long Retreat from the Hollow Wars. Its founders — the Cleric-Smith Aldred III, his sister Verena Brightaxe, and a company of fifty-three — had no quarry for the keep's stone within easy travel; instead they shaped its inner halls from a natural cavern system, leaving the visible exterior a low, weather-bitten ring of basalt walls atop the cliff face.

For two centuries Forgewatch served as a way-station for pilgrims traveling the Anvil's Road and as a checkpoint against incursions from the bandit-petty-kings of the Sunfall Marshes. The keep's bell — Old Resolute, cast at the founding — rang nightly at sunset for a continuous 134 years before the Silent Year of 1116, when no record explains its silence.

The current garrison numbers around sixty souls under Captain-Cleric Brother Aldric, kin (distantly) to the keep's founder. Forgewatch maintains close relations with the village of Silverbrook (1.5 days' march south) and a watchful enmity with the goblin warbands of the upper passes. The keep's inner sanctum, the Forgehouse, holds a working anvil at which all consecrations of dwarven steel for the Order are still performed.
