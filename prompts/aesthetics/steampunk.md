# Steampunk

Iron Kingdoms. Eberron. Dishonored. Ticking clockwork, brass fittings, walnut paneling, leather strap accents. Industrial precision under polite Victorian veneer.

## Ambiance adjectives

- brass, walnut, leather
- mechanical, ticking, gear-driven
- precisely engineered, riveted
- gas-lit, slightly smoke-stained
- analytical, instrumented
- restrained ornament

## Mature-tool style refs

- Dishonored's UI (gauges, dials)
- Bioshock's ammo counters and overlays
- Iron Kingdoms' source books (mechanical iconography)

## Color/material palette (in prose)

A rich walnut brown (`#3a2a1c`) for surfaces, brass (`#b48b3c`) for primary accents, gunmetal (`#3a3f44`) for secondary, ivory (`#e8dec7`) for displays under glass. Red is `rune-red` reserved for warnings — a brass dial showing red is *broken*, not decorative.

Texture: machined-metal sheens on accents, walnut grain on surfaces, leather creases on bordered regions. Heavy use of rivets at structural seams.

## Typographic intent

- Display: a sturdy slab serif (Roboto Slab, or Clarendon-style) — engineered, not decorative.
- Body: a humanist sans (Inter, or a Victorian sans like Trade Gothic). Tabular numerics throughout.
- Numerals: lining and tabular. Important values in monospace where possible (gauge readouts).
- Iconography: gear, valve, dial, gauge — all stroke-matched, with rivets where two pieces meet.

## Motion intent

- Mechanical. Snap-to states, not eased glides.
- Page transitions: a brass shutter slide.
- Dice: a roll with a satisfying brass-on-walnut clack; minimal bounce.
- Spell cast: clockwork wind-up + release.
- A faint ambient "tick" should be available as an option (off by default), never auto-on.

## Anti-patterns

- **Smooth flat surfaces.** Every panel wants beveled edges, rivets, or a leather strap.
- **Pastel colors.** This aesthetic has no pastel.
- **Sans-serif display headings.** The display face is engineered slab.
- **Overstated steam vapors.** Steam is a hint, not a fog. UI obscured by vapor reads as broken.
