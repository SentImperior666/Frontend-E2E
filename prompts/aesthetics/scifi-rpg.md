# Sci-fi RPG

Lancer. Cyberpunk RED. Mothership. Cold metallic surfaces, holographic affordances, technical readouts, corporate branding bleeding into UI.

## Ambiance adjectives

- carbon-fiber, brushed steel, anodized
- holographic, scanline, glitch (sparingly)
- diagnostic, instrumented, console-driven
- corporate, sterile, clinical
- nocturnal city, neon backlight

## Mature-tool style refs

- Lancer rulebook UI (mech HUD)
- Death Stranding's interface
- Alien: Isolation's terminals

## Color/material palette (in prose)

A near-black surface (`#0c0e10`) with cool-grey panels (`#171a1d`). Primary accent is a cyan (`#3ec1d3`) for active states, with a danger amber (`#e8a93b`) for warnings. Avoid green-on-black "matrix" cliché unless the brief explicitly leans into it.

Texture: subtle scanlines on data displays, faint anisotropic shimmer on metal surfaces, slight chromatic aberration around holographic affordances.

## Typographic intent

- Display: a technical sans (Eurostile-derivative, or a clean geometric like JetBrains Sans display).
- Body: a clean sans (Inter, IBM Plex Sans). Slightly increased line-height for diagnostic readability.
- Numerals: tabular, monospace for telemetry. Always.
- Iconography: stroke-based, single-weight, technical glyphs.

## Motion intent

- Snap, blink, scan. Snappy motion for state changes.
- Page transitions: a horizontal scan-wipe.
- Dice: a digital readout with a rapid spin-down (200–400ms).
- Spell / ability feedback: a holographic burst at affordance.
- Glitch effects: rare, only on damage or critical-failure feedback. Never as default decoration.

## Anti-patterns

- **Warm light, candle-glow.** This aesthetic has no warmth in chrome — warmth appears only in narrative imagery.
- **Decorative borders.** Borders are functional: a status indicator, a sensor frame.
- **Mixed-weight typography in dense panels.** Diagnostic reads want consistency.
- **Sci-fi "future fonts" with novelty letterforms.** Stay clean.
